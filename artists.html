Biography

Years Attended: 13

Burning man projects and affiliations:

Wylbur has built several LED-based art pieces. The Blinky Mirror was 4’x6’ interactive 1500-LED mirror that enabled dancing participants to paint their movements onto a smoothly diffused screen as random shadow effects created a sense of digital grain (2015-2019). Wallpaper was a 7'x4' panel displaying 12 randomly-initialized, non-repeating patterns across a grid of 960 LEDs behind a diffusion sheet (shown on the 9 o'c plaza in 2019 and 2022). Both the Mirror and Wallpaper were controlled with Raspberry Pi's driving Fadecandy boards. He has built several teensy-based wearable LED pieces, including a coat with interactivity driven by a capacitive touch sensor, and an illuminated fox tail that wags with a bit of petting (i.e., a double-click). Of course he has added every manner of camp- and bike-blinky decoration to JOBI, using every scrap of LED strand and stray arduino that has been left behind.
